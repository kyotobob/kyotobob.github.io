Linus Torvalds

While many immediately associate Linux with it's namesake, Linus Torvalds was also the inventor of Git. These two tools are used by a large percentage of programmers to maintian a safe, stable development environment. Mr.Torvalds is also an outspoken advocate of the open source movement.

Steve Wozniak

From designing one of the most influential computer systems to pioneering an organization dedicated to protecting digital freedom and online civil liberties, Steve Wozniak has proven to be a driven genius, a vocal critic, and a celebrated philanthropist.

Ken Thompson and Dennis Ritchie

These two are responsible for creating the languages that gave birth to modern computer language and the operating system that gave birth to modern operating sytems. A derivative of these two mens work can be found in almost any computational device.

Shafi Goldwasser and Silvio Micali

The founders of modern cryptography, these to study buddies codified the rules that govern modern cryptography, granting secure online transmission to the masses.

Tim Berners-Lee

Say hello to the father of the Web. While working at CERN, Tim Berners-Lee proposed linking several already developed technologies together ( file transfer protocol, transmission control protocol, extensive font sets, linked documents ) to create a method of communicating very detailed concepts. He developed this method into a web server at CERN and the World Wide Web was born. This saint of the electronic age also created the World Wide Web Consortium ( W3C ), which he still operates.

Richard Stallman

Father of Freeware, Saint IGNUcius himself. Stallman created a very influential free software activist organization called the Free Software Foundation. He also launched the GNU Project, a mass collection of free software created to work with the Unix system to offer a variety of solutions to users for free.
